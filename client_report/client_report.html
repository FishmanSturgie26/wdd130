Client Report

I wanted a Oscars website because the Award Ceremony was coming up. I originally wanted it to be every Oscar Winning Movie of every year since 2000, but as I was getting the posters and synopsis' I relized that it would take too much time for both me and the lead developer.

We emailed each other, not the best idea. When I sent out the first email, my client didn't even know he got one from me until the next day in class. Since then comunication was easy because we were both paying more attention to our emails.

I think my lead developer lead and directed perfectly. He made my website look exactly my site plan. He even added some extra flare that I really enjoyed.

Next time I will plan out a better way to communicate so my dev's can see my messages faster and without confusion.

Lead Developer Report

I again communicated via email, same issue I had when I was the client. At first it was hard finding my client's info and sharing it with my Jr. dev's, but we got it figured out the next day and didn't have any issues.

I took me awhile to figure out how to publish the site. But when I did, I was so happy it was that simple. I shared my github to show him the finish product, and I also sent him an email of the link.

I think it went really well. Both my Jr Dev's already knew and worked well with each other, and one of my Jr. Dev's was way better than me at making websites, so they both did really well.

Jr. Dev Reports

Again, mostly email. Had the same issue for all my groups, but also fixed it pretty quickly with all of them. I also had a hard time trying to join one of their github repositories, but they were also able to help me out in class.

For one site their wasn't a super solid site plan to work off of, but the lead developer took some creative liberties to make the website look like a website, and I just went along with what the Home Page looked like. The other site... the client never gave us anything beside an idea and our team just went with it including me.

General Experience

The most challenging was deffinitly the Lead Dev. I have a hard time programming in general but I was lucky to have such great Jr. Devs to support me.

The most enjoyable aspect was creating my website outline as a client and to see it come to life as a finished product!

I would try to get more done ahead of time instead of doing most of my stuff all at once.

I think after this experience, I will deffinitly feel more confident. I know now the best way to communicate is anything other than email, and how to better communicate and work with my fellow devs!!
